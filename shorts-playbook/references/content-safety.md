# Content Mode & Claim Safety

The #1 legal/platform risk in shopping shorts is **claiming a result you can't back up** — especially skincare / health / beauty ("this fixed my dark circles", "it lifted my face in 7 days"). What a script is allowed to SAY must be gated on whether the creator actually used the product. This guardrail sits ABOVE persona/hook: pick the Content Mode first, then write within its allowed voice.

## Content Mode — choose one per short, default to a SAFE mode

| Mode | Use when | Allowed voice | Never say |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Direct Review** | you actually bought / filmed / used it | "I tried…", "my routine", "after using…" | medical / treatment / guaranteed-result claims |
| **Curated Find** *(default-safe)* | NOT personally used | "trending", "worth checking", "people are comparing" | "I used", "it fixed", "guaranteed" |
| **Deal Alert** | discount / coupon focus | "on sale", "coupon available" | efficacy claims |
| **Review Summary** | summarizing public reviews | "many reviewers mention…" | speaking as your own experience |
| **Comparison** | comparing similar products | "compared with", "check before buying" | unsupported superiority |

**Default = Curated Find or Deal Alert, never Direct Review.** Only unlock Direct Review when real first-hand footage/asset is attached to the content.

## Claim Safety — risky → safe rewrite

Downgrade any result claim to an observation unless Direct Review is active:

- "I used this for 7 days and my skin changed." → "This tool is trending with people looking for an at-home routine."
- "This lifted my face." → "It's marketed as a facial-lifting-style device — check the details and reviews before buying."
- "This fixed my dark circles." → "Many viewers are discussing it for under-eye concerns, but results can vary."

Rules: no medical / treatment verbs, no guaranteed outcomes, no first-person result claims outside Direct Review. When unsure, use "marketed as…", "people are discussing…", "results can vary". Health/beauty categories are high-risk — flag and soften by default.

## Originality Transform — keep the function, change the surface

Take the reference reel's **purpose** (hook archetype, beat order, pacing, CTA mechanic). Change the **wording, visual composition, creator persona, and product framing**. Never reproduce the original's sentences or its shot-for-shot look.

- "remake script" is a trap — treat it as an **Inspired Structure Script**.
- Analyze each scene's **marketing function**, not its literal content (**Creative Function Analysis**).
- Generate several **Hook Variants** (per platform), not one copied hook.

## Platform Export — same product, different surface

| Platform | Hook | CTA | Format |
|---|---|---|---|
| **TikTok** | fast, hard problem in first 1s | TikTok Shop / comment / link | 15–35s, fast cuts, comment-driven |
| **Instagram** | pretty visual, save-worthy | story link, profile link, save/share | Reel + carousel + highlight reuse |
| **YouTube Shorts** | informational / series hook | full review or series link | 30–60s, title/series consistency |

## Sellability & purchase-intent (feeds product selection, not the VO)

A product is worth a short when: impulse-buy price (coupon/discount is hook material), credible rating/reviews, an effect that's **easy to show on camera in ~3s**, and comment signals of purchase intent ("link?", "need this", "where to buy?", "price?"). Low claim-risk raises priority. This scores the product BEFORE scripting.
