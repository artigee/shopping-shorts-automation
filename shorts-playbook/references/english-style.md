# US English Style & Compliance

VO and captions are authored natively in US English. This file holds the register and the compliance norms that make the output sound American and stay platform-safe.

## Register

- Conversational, spoken — contractions always ("didn't," "it's," "I'd"). Write for the ear, not the page.
- Deadpan and understatement read as confident; over-exclamation reads as a bot. Prefer a dry "that felt illegal" over "this is AMAZING!!"
- Idioms should be US-native: "eight bucks a scoop," "for once," "the whole trick is," "no rock-salt situation."
- Small confessions and asides are the voice ("I timed it, because of course I did").

## Dollar / value references

- Use concrete US dollar amounts, framed in the persona's voice, not as a sales pitch. "I used to pay eight bucks a scoop" — personal, specific. NOT "save money with this Amazon find."
- "Amazon find" is banned (it's the generic tell). Reference price/value in the persona's own words instead.

## CTA mechanic (US platforms)

- Comment-keyword → DM-link is the standard soft funnel (TikTok Shop / IG). Keep the CTA caption short ("Comment NEED IT 👇").
- The VO close is casual, not a hard sell ("Say the word and it's in your DMs").

## FTC disclosure (affiliate / Amazon Associates)

US affiliate content requires a clear, conspicuous disclosure. Bake it into the deliverable so it isn't retrofitted per video:

- Include a visible disclosure (e.g. on-screen "#ad" or "Commissions earned," and/or spoken) — not buried.
- "As an Amazon Associate I earn from qualifying purchases" is the standard Associates line where a written disclosure is used.
- Keep disclosure language factual; it is exempt from the persona voice and the ban-list (it must stay literal and clear).

This skill flags disclosure as a required field on the CTA in the beat sheet, but the exact placement/wording is the creator's call and should be checked against current platform + FTC guidance.
