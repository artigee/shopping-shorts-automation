# Worked Example — Dash My Pint (single-serve ice cream maker)

This is the calibration asset. The structure is identical in both versions below;
only the VO changed. Read both VO columns aloud — the difference is the whole point.

Persona used for the good version: `skeptic_won_over`.

## The flat version (what generic VO looks like — DO NOT ship)

| Time | Caption (claim) | VO (flat — narrates the caption) |
|------|-----------------|----------------------------------|
| 0–2s | 20-minute ice cream. No shop. | This Amazon find makes ice cream in 20 minutes at home. |
| 2–3s | Single-serve. No ice, no salt. | It's a single-serve maker with no ice and no salt needed. |
| 3–5s | Add your cream base | Add your favorite cream base into the bowl. |
| 5–6s | Your mix-ins 🍓 | Add whatever toppings you like. |
| 6–7.5s | Lid on. One button. | Put the lid on and press one button. |
| 7.5–8s | 20 min ⏱ | Twenty minutes later it's done. |
| 8–9s | Real soft-serve 🍦 | It comes out thick, creamy, way better than store-bought. |
| 9–10s | Comment NEED IT 👇 | Comment below and I'll drop the link straight to your DMs. |

Why it's flat: every VO line restates its caption, it reaches for "Amazon find" /
"way better than" / "drop the link" (all banned), and it has no point of view.

## The good version (react, don't narrate)

Continuous monologue, written FIRST:

> "I did not believe a twenty-minute ice cream machine was a real thing. The
> whole trick is one bowl that just lives in the freezer — that's it. You pour
> your cream in, and this is where I kept waiting for the catch. Throw in
> whatever you've got; I did strawberries. And then you just… leave. That's the
> part that felt illegal. Twenty minutes — I timed it, because of course I did.
> And it's genuinely good: scoops clean, tastes like the real thing, not sad
> frozen milk. Say the word and it's in your DMs."
